1. Abstract

This paper presents Hola-TTS, a novel cross-lingual zero-shot text-to-speech system capable of synthesizing speech in Chinese, English, Japanese, and Korean.

We introduce several key innovations, including a comprehensive data cleaning process, a multilingual phoneme representation strategy, and an improved model architecture. Hola-TTS employs a fully autoregressive method for token generation and utilizes an end mask approach, which significantly reduces common issues in zero-shot TTS such as long silences, prompt leakage, and speaker confusion. Our model is trained on a diverse set of cleaned speech recognition datasets and uses phoneme-level language label to enhance cross-lingual synthesis capabilities.

Comparative evaluations demonstrate that Hola-TTS outperforms state-of-the-art models like VALL-E X and X-TTS across various metrics. Ablation studies further confirm the effectiveness of our proposed improvements compare to VoiceCraft. This work represents a significant advancement in multilingual speech synthesis, offering improved quality, consistency, and cross-lingual capabilities in zero-shot TTS.
